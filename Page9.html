Technical Writing

Number credits: 2 (2-0-0)

Theory/ Lab: Theory

Time of the class: Thursday 4:30-5:30

Class Room: A-222

Instructor Information

Instructor Name: Dr. Sarah Johnson

Course Objectives

  • To develop proficiency in writing clear, concise, and effective technical documents
  • To understand the principles of organizing and presenting technical information
  • To learn how to adapt writing style for different audiences and purposes
  • To practice various forms of technical communication including reports, proposals, and presentations

Course Outcome

  • Ability to write clear and effective technical documents
  • Proficiency in organizing and structuring technical information
  • Skills in adapting writing style for different technical contexts
  • Experience in creating various types of technical documents
  • Understanding of ethical considerations in technical writing

Modules

Module 1: Fundamentals of Technical Writing

Introduction to technical writing, Audience analysis, Writing style and tone

Module 2: Document Design and Structure

Document organization, Visual elements, Writing effective paragraphs and sentences

Module 3: Types of Technical Documents

Technical reports, Proposals, User manuals, White papers

Module 4: Advanced Topics

Technical presentations, Ethics in technical writing, Collaborative writing
